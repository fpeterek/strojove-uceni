KMeans appears to have yielded better results. Cluster 1 contains a lot of smaller teams and Atlético Madrid, hinting perhaps at a more defensive
style of play with few goals scored. Smaller teams rarely stand a chance in the Champions League. Atlético Madrid likes to play defensively even
if they're a big team. The Italians are also known for a defensive style of play, which would explain the presence of the 2009/2010 squads of
Juventus and Inter.

Clusters 4, 5, 6 and 8 are similar to cluster 1. They contain predominantly smaller teams or teams that play defensively.

Clusters 7 and 9 contain many big names and successful squads.

When examining clusters 2 and 10, we may notice FC Barcelona getting clustered with Bayern Munich and Manchester City quite a lot, again, possibly
due to a similar style of gameplay. All three of these clubs have, at a certain point in time, been coached by Pep Guardiola. The tactics of those
three teams could thus draw inspiration from Johan Cruyff. Pep Guardiola managed Barcelona until the year 2012. Between the years 2013-2016, Guardiola
managed Bayern Munchen, and the 2013/2014 Bayern squad is present in cluster 2. Since 2016, Guardiola has been the manager of Manchester City, and thus,
later City squads can be seen clustered among Barcelona and Bayern squads.

VfB Stuttgart appears to be a lonely outlier, despite not doing bad in the 2009/2010 season of Champions League and advancing to the round of 16, finishing
their CL run with a goal difference of -1.
